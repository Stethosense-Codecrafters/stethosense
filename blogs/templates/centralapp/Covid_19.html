Corona Checkpoints.
Keep the following things in mind.

A mask is a must.

Wear a fabric mask unless you’re in a particular risk group. This is especially important when you can’t stay physically distanced, particularly in crowded and poorly ventilated indoor settings. Wear a medical/surgical mask if you:
  • Are over 60
  • Have underlying medical conditions
  • Are feeling unwell
  • Are looking after an ill family member

Wash your hands often

Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds especially after you have been in a public place, or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing. It’s especially important to wash:
  • Before eating or preparing food
  • Before touching your face
  • After leaving a public place
  • After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing
  • After handling your mask
  • After changing a diaper
  • After caring for someone sick
  • After touching animals or pets

Remember the 6 Feet Rule.

Always ensure a minimum gap of 2 meters (or 6 feet) between you and others. This ensures that the droplet spread of this disease is minimised. Keeping distance from others is especially important for people who are at higher risk of getting very sick.

Avoid the 3Cs: spaces that are closed, crowded or involve close contact.

The risks of getting COVID-19 are higher in crowded and inadequately ventilated spaces where infected people spend long periods of time together in close proximity.
These environments are where the virus appears to spreads by respiratory droplets or aerosols more efficiently, so taking precautions is even more important.

Always keep a sanitiser handy

If soap and water are not readily available, use a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol. Cover all surfaces of your hands and rub them together until they feel dry.
The picture on the left shows how to use a sanitiser correctly.

Cover cough and sneezes

Follow the right etiquettes while coughing and sneezing. Always use a tissue or a cloth to cover your mouth while sneezing. If a tissue is not available, use your elbow to cover your mouth. Never spit.
Throw used tissues in the trash only.

Disinfect regularly

Clean AND disinfect frequently touched surfaces daily. This includes tables, doorknobs, light switches, countertops, handles, desks, phones, keyboards, toilets, faucets, and sinks.
Use soap and water prior to disinfection if the surfaces are dirty.

Watch out for symptoms

Check if you or your family members have any of the following symptoms.
  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatique
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Muscle or body ache
  • Diarrhea
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Headache
If you suspect chances of having come in contact with a Coronavirus patient, seek medical help immediately.
